Animation & motion intensity?

How much does the page move? This affects polish, implementation complexity, and how premium it feels.

A

Subtle — Tasteful micro-interactions

What moves: Cards fade + slide up when scrolled into view. Buttons scale on hover. Pain bubbles pulse gently. Counters count up on scroll.

Tech: Pure CSS transitions + Intersection Observer. No libs.

Feels like: Stripe, Linear, Zerodha — restrained and professional. Every animation has a purpose.

B

Medium — Scroll-driven storytelling ⭐ Recommended

What moves: Pain bubbles float + cycle (CSS keyframes). Hero chaos → order transition on scroll. Feature sections alternate slide-in (left/right). Pricing cards stagger in. Smooth section transitions.

Tech: CSS keyframes + Framer Motion (lightweight). ~15KB addition.

Feels like: Cred, Razorpay, early Notion — expressive and alive, but never gimmicky. This is the sweet spot for Indian consumer apps.

C

Heavy — Cinematic scroll experience

What moves: Parallax hero layers. Scroll-snapped full-screen sections. Morphing backgrounds between sections. 3D card flips. Text scramble effects. Lottie illustrations.

Tech: GSAP ScrollTrigger + Lottie + custom shaders. Significant complexity.

Feels like: Apple product pages, Vercel, Linear's hero — stunning but takes weeks to build and can hurt performance + mobile experience.

The current site has zero animations. Even option A would be a massive upgrade. I recommend B — it delivers the "next level" feel without the implementation overhead of C.
